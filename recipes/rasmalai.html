Traditional Rasmalai

Description

Rasmalai is a popular Indian dessert made with soft and spongy chenna (paneer) balls soaked in rich, creamy saffron and cardamom flavored milk, garnished with nuts. It's festive, elegant, and melts in the mouth!

Ingredients

  • 1 liter full cream milk (for chenna)
  • 2 tablespoons lemon juice (diluted with 2 tbsp water)
  • 1 cup sugar (for syrup)
  • 4 cups water
  • 500 ml full cream milk (for ras)
  • 2-3 tablespoons sugar (for ras)
  • 4-5 saffron strands
  • 1/2 teaspoon cardamom powder
  • Chopped almonds and pistachios

Steps

  1. Boil 1 liter milk, add lemon juice to curdle. Strain and rinse chenna (paneer). Hang for 30 minutes.
  2. Knead chenna until smooth and make small flattened balls.
  3. Boil 1 cup sugar and 4 cups water in a deep pan. Add chenna balls and cook covered for 15 minutes on high heat.
  4. In another pan, boil 500 ml milk and reduce slightly. Add saffron, sugar, cardamom powder, and nuts.
  5. Take out chenna balls, squeeze gently, and soak in the prepared milk (ras).
  6. Chill for a few hours and serve cold, garnished with more nuts and saffron.
